French expatriate in Thailand since May 2019, my wife and I chose to change our lives.

For me, it is a chance to live my dream, live in my way with my passions: running, yoga, surfing, and meditation for a few years.

I started running in Brittany, for the pleasure of spending my energy by communing with the elements, on the coastal trails.
Then, once landed in the Paris area, I continued to practice to "escape".
At 26, I ran my first marathon.
Like many people, it was just "the story of running one, at least once in a lifetime ..." and especially because I needed to prove myself.

I'm 39, I've been running for over 20 years now and the running adventure continues but my practice, my approach have evolved.
Even if the chrono is still current, guided by the desire to challenge myself but without being forced, this is a philosophy of life.

Since 2011, I complete the practice by devoting an increasingly important part to yoga and meditation, which considerably improves recovery, energy management and the state of mindfulness during the training phases and obviously in everyday life.

Over the years, running advices given to my former coworkers and friends, especially in preparation for the marathon, putted in advance a pedagogue side and a conciliatory nature.

I am ready to give you the tools to go-back with an "uninhibited" vision of running, for a sustainable healthy and balanced practice, for your body and your mind.

Greg
