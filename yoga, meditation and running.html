Yoga, meditation, 
mindfulness and running


Without realizing, I started yoga practice in 2011, with simple exercices in the aim to prevent recurrent pains due to a sciatique.
I decided to act.
Since then, I practice every day, this contributes to my balance and promotes a better perception of myself.

Meanwhile, valuable advice from an osteopath specialized in running helped me to literally transform my stride to better absorb repeated shocks in the lower back and around  the pelvis, related to running and trail running.
He also made me aware of the importance of our stride, which can be more efficient and adaptable according to the type of ground, the collapse of our posture as soon as the first signs of fatigue appear, and the importance of abdominal exercices.
These sessions finished by easy stretches and exercises to put in place at home to treat the runner's minor pains




In 2015, during an initiation, I realized that I was already practicing yoga postures, I just had to focus more on breathing.
Thereafter, a yoga teacher friend helped me to consolidate my basis and guided me towards meditation. 
Since 2017, Yoga and meditation contribute to my daily balance and they are integral parts of my preparations, especially in recovery phases. 
I breathe better and I literally take the time to breathe, which allows me to be more lucid and gradually improves the transition to the states of mindfulness.  
These practices necessarily had a positive influence in my change of life !  
During my training and generally during my preparations, the effort management is more regular,
the recovery is much better and my race results remain constant over the years.
I channel my energy expenditure to avoid overtraining, a phase conducive to underperformance and the appearance of injuries. 

My postures are simple and accessible to everyone.

It involves stretching and flexibility exercices made according to listening to the body and especially depending on the desire.

I don't impose anything on myself.

The goal is to regenerate themselves by going to feel the tensions, accept them and relieve them at the rate of breathing.

Finally, my sister, with her spiritual vision, guides me about the importance of our energy balance,
listening to our inner self.

Improve your daily life, apply yoga in your life !
